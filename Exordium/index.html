About The Event

About The Event

IEEE Student Branch GEC Barton Hill, Trivandrum is proud to announce it’s first ever Flagship Event - EXORDIUM. Featuring a plethora of Talks, Competitions and Workshops, Exordium will provide you with several opportunities to unleash your inner scholar and tech-wiz. Along with this, there’ll be several opportunities to bag awesome prizes! Don’t miss out, join us from September 10th for the opportunity of a lifetime.

Where

GECBH,TRIVANDRUM

When

Monday to Friday
10-30 September

  • Day 1

Registration

Flutter GOKUL C M

IEEE SB GECBH in collaboration with Agora and GADS LLP presents a Workshop on Introduction to Flutter and Agora App Builder associated with the Flagship Event EXORDIUM.

Augmented Reality

Shyam Pradeep Anil

IEEE Computer Society SBC GEC Barton Hill, Trivandrum in collaboration with IEEE Young Professionals, Kerala Section is here to present you with a Talk on Augmented Reality as part of the Flagship Event EXORDIUM, organized by IEEE Student Branch GEC Barton Hill, Trivandrum. Publishing a Research Paper in IEEE Dr. Jisha V R

02:00 PM

The Woman Entrepreneur Ms. Sofia Mathew

IEEE WIE AG GEC Barton Hill, Trivandrum presents A talk in association with our flagship event EXORDIUM - "The Woman Entrepreneur-Can she have it all?" Make sure to utilise this amazing chance to acquire knowledge from the finest!

03:00 PM

RF Energy Harvesting Systems Ms. Sleebi K Divakaran

IEEE APS and MTT-S SBCs GEC Barton Hill, Trivandrum present a talk in association with our flagship event EXORDIUM- RF Energy Harvesting Systems: An Overview. We present to you an opportunity to learn from the best of the best. So don't miss it!

04:00 PM

surface electromagnetics Prof. Fan Yang

The session will be handled by Prof. Fan Yang, Professor, Microwave and Antenna Institute Electronic Engineering Department, Tsinghua University, Beijing, China. He has published six books, seven book chapters, and over 400 journal articles and conference papers with his research interests in antennas, surface electromagnetics, computational electromagnetics, and applied electromagnetic systems.
